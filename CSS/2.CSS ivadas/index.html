ARCTIC FOX


The Arctic fox lives in some of the most frigid extremes on the planet, but they do not start to shiver until the temperature drops to −70 °C.

Scientific name: Vulpes lagopus
Body length: 46-68 cm
Mass: 3.2-9.4 kg
Lifespan: up to 11 years

The Arctic fox preys on many small creatures. In the wild, most individuals do not live past their first year. The Arctic fox is well adapted to living in cold environments, their warm fur is also used as camouflage.
